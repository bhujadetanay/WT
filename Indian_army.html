Overview

Role & Responsibility

The Indian Army is the land-based branch of the Indian Armed Forces. It is responsible for defending the nation against external aggression and maintaining internal security when required.

Terrain & Readiness

From glaciers of Siachen to deserts of Rajasthan and dense jungles, the Indian Army operates across the most diverse terrains in the world.

Structure & Equipment

Armoured Corps

The Armoured Corps forms the backbone of mechanised warfare, equipped with modern tanks capable of high mobility and firepower.

Artillery Regiment

Artillery units provide long-range fire support and play a crucial role in both defensive and offensive operations.

Special Forces

The PARA SF are elite soldiers trained for unconventional warfare, counter-terrorism, reconnaissance, and strategic missions.

Major Operations & Contributions

Historic Operations

The Indian Army has played a decisive role in major conflicts such as the 1947 war, 1971 Bangladesh Liberation War, Kargil conflict, and numerous counter-insurgency operations.

United Nations Missions

India is one of the largest contributors to UN peacekeeping missions, with Indian Army personnel serving in conflict zones across the globe.
