Introduction

Indian Army

The Indian Army is the land-based branch of the Indian Armed Forces and is one of the largest standing armies in the world. It was formally established after India gained independence in 1947 and operates under the Ministry of Defence.

Its primary mission is to ensure national security and defend the sovereignty and territorial integrity of India against external threats and internal challenges.

Primary Responsibilities

The Indian Army is responsible for conducting land warfare operations, maintaining internal security when required, and assisting civil authorities during natural disasters such as floods, earthquakes, and pandemics.

It also plays a vital role in peacekeeping missions under the United Nations, contributing to global stability.

Operational Reach

From glaciers to deserts, the Army operates in some of the most challenging environments on Earth.

Organizational Structure

Command Structure

The Indian Army is headed by the Chief of the Army Staff (COAS). Operationally, it is divided into several commands such as Northern, Southern, Eastern, Western, Central, and South Western Commands.

Each command is responsible for security in a specific geographical region of the country.

Major Arms & Services

The Army consists of various arms including Infantry, Armoured Corps, Artillery, Engineers, Signals, Army Aviation, and supporting services such as Medical Corps and Logistics.

These units work together to ensure coordinated and effective military operations.

Key Missions and Operations

Indo-Pak War

1947-1948

The first conflict after independence, fought to secure territorial integrity and establish national defense capabilities.

Bangladesh Liberation War

1971

A decisive military operation that led to the creation of Bangladesh and demonstrated India's strategic and humanitarian role.

Operation Blue Star

1984

An internal security operation conducted to restore law and order in Punjab during a period of insurgency.

Kargil Conflict

1999

A high-altitude conflict where Indian forces successfully reclaimed strategic positions along the Line of Control.

Training and Discipline

Military Training

Training in the Indian Army is rigorous and continuous. Soldiers are trained in physical endurance, weapon handling, battlefield tactics, leadership, and survival skills.

Training institutions such as the Indian Military Academy (IMA), Officers Training Academy (OTA), and various regimental centres prepare personnel for diverse operational roles.

Code of Values

The Indian Army follows a strict code of discipline based on values such as integrity, loyalty, courage, selfless service, and respect for the nation and its citizens.

The motto “Service Before Self” reflects the spirit of sacrifice and dedication expected from every soldier.

Humanitarian and Peacekeeping Role

Disaster Relief

The Indian Army plays a crucial role during natural disasters by conducting rescue operations, providing medical aid, and restoring essential services.

UN Peacekeeping

India is among the largest contributors to United Nations peacekeeping forces, with Indian Army personnel deployed in conflict zones worldwide.
