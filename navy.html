History & Overview

Established: 5 September 1612

As the East India Company’s Marine to protect maritime trade routes and was later known as the Royal Indian Navy during British rule. After India’s independence in 1947, it was renamed the Indian Navy in 1950 when India became a republic. The Indian Navy is the maritime arm of the Indian Armed Forces and is responsible for safeguarding India’s vast coastline, territorial waters, and maritime interests. It plays a crucial role in national security, anti-piracy operations, disaster relief, and humanitarian missions.


With the motto “Sham No Varunah” (May the Lord of the Oceans be Auspicious to Us), the Indian Navy stands as a symbol of strength, vigilance, and maritime power.

Key Missions & Operations

Year Operation Name Description
1971 Operation Trident Naval missile attack on Karachi harbour during Indo-Pak War.
1971 Operation Python Follow-up strike causing major damage to enemy naval assets.
1988 Operation Cactus Rapid deployment to Maldives to stop a coup attempt.
1984 Operation Meghdoot Capturing the Siachen Glacier, the world's highest battlefield.
1999 Operation Talwar Naval blockade of Pakistan during the Kargil War.
2020 Operation Samudra Setu Repatriation of Indians stranded abroad during COVID-19.
