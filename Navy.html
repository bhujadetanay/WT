Proud to be an Indian

The Indian Navy oath is a solemn pledge of allegiance to the Indian Constitution, promising faithful service in the naval forces, obeying the President and superiors, even at the peril of one's life, and going wherever ordered by land, sea, or air, embodying loyalty and duty to the nation's maritime defense. Key components of the Oath: Allegiance: To bear true faith and allegiance to the Constitution of India. Service: To serve honestly and faithfully in the naval service. Obedience: To observe and obey all commands of the President and superior officers. Commitment: To go wherever ordered (sea, land, or air) and to risk life in duty.
