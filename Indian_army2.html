History & Overview

Established: 1 April 1895

The Indian Army is the land-based branch of the Indian Armed Forces. It is responsible for defending the nation against external aggression and maintaining internal security when required.

After independence in 1947, the Indian Army evolved into a modern, professional force with a strong emphasis on discipline, training, and operational readiness.

Army Commands

Major Regiments & Equipment

Infantry

The backbone of the Indian Army, responsible for frontline combat.

Armoured Corps

Equipped with modern tanks such as the T-90 Bhishma and Arjun MBT.

Artillery

Provides long-range fire support using howitzers and rocket systems.

Special Forces (PARA SF)

Elite units trained for counter-terrorism and special operations.
