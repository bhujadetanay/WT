History & Overview

Established: 8 October 1932

The Indian Air Force (IAF) is the air arm of the Indian Armed Forces. Its complement of personnel and aircraft assets ranks fourth amongst the air forces of the world. Its primary mission is to secure Indian airspace and to conduct aerial warfare during armed conflict.


From its humble beginnings as an auxiliary air force of the British Empire with four Westland Wapiti biplanes, the IAF has evolved into a modern, technologically advanced force capable of precision strikes and strategic airlift.

Organization Structure

The IAF is organized into 7 commands (5 Operational, 2 Functional).

Western Air Command

HQ: New Delhi

Eastern Air Command

HQ: Shillong, Meghalaya

Central Air Command

HQ: Prayagraj, UP

South Western Air Command

HQ: Gandhinagar, Gujarat

Southern Air Command

HQ: Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala

Training Command

HQ: Bengaluru, Karnataka

Maintenance Command

HQ: Nagpur, Maharashtra

Major Aircraft & Vehicles

Dassault Rafale

Role: Multi-role Combat Aircraft

Origin: France

Twin-engine, canard delta wing, multirole fighter aircraft equipped with a wide range of weapons.

Sukhoi Su-30MKI

Role: Air Superiority Fighter

Origin: Russia/India (HAL)

The backbone of the IAF. A twinjet multirole air superiority fighter developed by Russia's Sukhoi.

HAL Tejas

Role: Light Combat Aircraft

Origin: India (Indigenous)

Single-engine, delta wing, light multirole fighter designed by the Aeronautical Development Agency.

C-17 Globemaster III

Role: Strategic Airlifter

Origin: USA

A large military transport aircraft used for rapid strategic airlift of troops and cargo.

Key Missions & Operations

Year Operation Name Description
1947 Kashmir Operations Transported troops to Srinagar to prevent invaders from seizing the airfield.
1961 Operation Vijay The annexation of Goa from Portuguese rule.
1971 Indo-Pak War Achieved air superiority in the East, aiding the creation of Bangladesh.
1984 Operation Meghdoot Capturing the Siachen Glacier, the world's highest battlefield.
1999 Operation Safed Sagar Use of air power at high altitude (Kargil) to flush out infiltrators.
2019 Operation Bandar Pre-emptive air strikes on terror camps in Balakot.
